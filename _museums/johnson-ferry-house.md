An early-1700s gambrel-roofed farmhouse and tavern in Washington Crossing State Park (NJ), built by ferry operator Garret Johnson and likely used by Washington's officers the night of the 1776 crossing. Period-furnished rooms, an 18th-century kitchen garden, and weekend living-history demonstrations — a distinct stop from the park's Visitor Center Museum.
